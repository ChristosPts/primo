Alignments

Use alignment classes to vertically and horizontally align columns.

Vertical alignments

The align-items and align-self properties control alignment of our flex items on the cross axis, down the columns if flex-direction is row and along the row if flex-direction is column.

                
<div class="row align-baseline"> ..... </div>                    
<div class="row align-start"> ..... </div>                    
<div class="row align-center"> ..... </div>                    
<div class="row align-end"> ..... </div>

Vertical self-alignments

Use align-self utilities on flexbox items to individually change their alignment on the cross axis.

                
<div class="col-* align-self-baseline"> ..... </div>                    
<div class="col-* align-self-start"> ..... </div>                    
<div class="col-* align-self-center"> ..... </div>                    
<div class="col-* align-self-end"> ..... </div>

Horizontal alignments

The justify property defines how the browser distributes space between and around content items along the main-axis of a flex container, and the inline axis of a grid container..

                
<div class="row display-f justify-start"> ..... </div>                    
<div class="row display-f justify-center"> ..... </div>                    
<div class="row display-f justify-end"> ..... </div>  
<div class="row display-f justify-space-between"> ..... </div>                    
<div class="row display-f justify-space-around"> ..... </div>                    
<div class="row display-f justify-space-evenly"> ..... </div>
