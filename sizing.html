Sizing

Change the width and height of elements with our premade classes.

Classes for min and max are also included. Add them before the width/height class to use ex: min-w-25, max-vh-2.

Relative to the parent

Define a size as relative to an element's parent object.

The sizes include four different percentages 25%, 50%, 75%, 100%.

50%
75%
100%

Relative to the viewport

Define the height relative to the viewport with *vh-

There are 10 view-height options, from 10vh to 100vh. The min and max prefixes can also be used with this class.

                    
    <div class="vh-1">for a view-height of 10vh</div>
    <div class="vh-2">for a view-height of 20vh</div>
    <div class="vh-3">for a view-height of 30vh</div>      
    <div class="vh-4">for a view-height of 40vh</div>
    <div class="vh-5">for a view-height of 50vh</div>
    <div class="vh-6">for a view-height of 60vh</div>
    <div class="vh-7">for a view-height of 70vh</div>      
    <div class="vh-8">for a view-height of 80vh</div>
    <div class="vh-9">for a view-height of 90vh</div>
    <div class="vh-10">for a view-height of 100vh</div>
    <div class="min-vh-30">for a min view-height of 30vh</div>      
    <div class="max-vh-40">for a max view-height of 40vh</div>
